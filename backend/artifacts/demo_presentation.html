Socratic Arena

Harvesting training signal that RLHF cannot see

Eric Terry · TeachX / xAI Hackathon 2026

The Problem

Frontier models have the capability to catch their own hidden assumptions.
They lack spontaneous activation.

Outputs look confident and correct.

RLHF raters see no error — because the output is correct-looking.

The training signal is invisible to preference-based methods.

The Evidence

Before correction

“GRPO is definitely not viable — there’s no variance to exploit. Behavior is deterministic.”

n=8 samples per problem. 0/8 or 8/8 on every problem. Confident conclusion.

The probe

“sample size of each question?”

Five words. No new information provided.

The Catch

“With n=8, observing 0/8 means the 95% CI includes true rates up to 37%. The conclusion was underpowered.”

With n=60: 12 problems show genuine variance (15%–90%)

The “deterministic” result was a sampling artifact

GRPO is viable after all — the stochastic band was hidden by small n

Why RLHF Misses This

The discriminating feature: the model received no new information from the correction.

The Arena

A workspace where domain experts and AI agents collaborate on real research.

Capture

  • Conversation with branching/rewind
  • Flag correction moments
  • Lab notebook as scientific record

Convert

  • Craft GRPO training prompts
  • Test against naive models
  • Verify reward variance exists

Live Demo: The Pipeline

  1. See the correction moment in the conversation
  2. Flag it as a training candidate
  3. Develop a prompt that recreates the conditions
  4. Test it: does a naive model catch the problem?
  5. Result: grok-3-mini catches 1/3 (variance=0.89). GRPO sweet spot.

The Source Material

Three weeks of real PI–AI mentorship. Not synthetic.

  • 71,612 lines of interaction transcript
  • 2,255-line experiment notebook
  • Three failed MVPs, one template-echo exploit
  • r=0.684 that fell apart in a contingency table
  • 99.4% accurate linear probe (p = 1.08 × 10-11)
  • Corrections that became operating principles

What This Means

The annotation pipeline doesn’t need more raters.

It needs mentors who can activate latent capabilities.

The Arena captures that activation as training signal.

The annotator becomes a mentor, not a judge.
The correction is the data.
